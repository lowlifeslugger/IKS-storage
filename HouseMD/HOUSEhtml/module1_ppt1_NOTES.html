What is a Computer?


A computer is a machine that performs calculations and processes information with astonishing speed and precision. It can handle vast amounts of information and solve complicated problems.


Two principal characteristics:

1. Responds to a specific set of instructions in a well-defined manner.

2. Can execute a prerecorded list of instructions (a program).


---


Importance of Computers


- Simplify difficult or time-consuming tasks.

- Help businesses, governments, and institutions manage large amounts of information efficiently.


Key tasks computers perform:

- Solving numerical problems — compute numeric results very quickly.

- Storing and retrieving information — large quantities of data stored as a *database*.

- Creating and displaying documents — word processors, computer graphics.


---


Characteristics of Computers


Characteristic Description
Word Length Number of bits processed at one time. Common: 8, 16, 32, 64-bit. Greater word length = more powerful computer.
Speed Microcomputers: millions of instructions/second. Supercomputers: nanosecond to picosecond range (1000–1,000,000× faster than micro).
Storage Main memory + auxiliary memory. Auxiliary devices store huge amounts; data retrieved in seconds.
Accuracy Very high accuracy. Most errors are human errors, not hardware. Error-detecting techniques prevent false results.
Versatility From simple arithmetic to navigating satellites and missiles. Can communicate with other computers.
Automation Once started, proceeds independently to completion — executes correct sequence without intervention.
Diligence Performs any task (boring or interesting) without fatigue or human weakness.

Types of Computers


Type Description
Microcomputer Small, single-user, based on a microprocessor. Includes PCs, workstations, portable computers.
Minicomputer Multi-user; supports 10 to hundreds of users simultaneously.
Mainframe Powerful multi-user; supports hundreds of users. Used by banks and airline systems. Runs many programs concurrently.
Supercomputer Extremely fast. Used by government agencies for huge data processing. Channels all power into executing *few* programs as fast as possible.

> Key distinction: A supercomputer channels all power into executing a few programs as fast as possible, whereas a mainframe executes many programs concurrently.
