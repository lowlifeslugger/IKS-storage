INTRODUCTION TO COMPUTERS
• A computer is a machine that performs calculations and processes information 
with astonishing speed and precision.
• It can handle vast amount of  information and handle complicated problems.
Two principal characteristics of  a computer are:
1. It responds to a specific set of  instructions in a well defined manner
2. It can execute a prerecorded list of  instructions(a program)

IMPORTANCE OF COMPUTERS
• Simplify difficult or time consuming tasks.
• Provide businesses, governments , individuals and institutions with an efficient way to 
manage large amount of  information
• It performs certain tasks quickly and accurately. These tasks include:
1. Solving numerical ability: computers can solve numeric problems very quickly.
2. Storing and retrieving information: People can use computers to store large quantities of  
information. Information stored in a computer is sometimes called as a database. 
3. Creating and displaying documents and pictures: Word processing program allows 
people to type words into a computer to write articles, books, letters, reports and other 
kinds of  documents. Computer graphics is use of  computers to make pictures.


## Page 5
CHARACTERISTICS OF COMPUTERS
Computers have the following characteristics:
1. Word length:
• A digital computer operates on binary digits 0 and 1. Binary digit is called a bit. Group of  8 bits is a byte.
• Word length: Number of  bits a computer can process at a time.
• Commonly used word lengths are 8,16,32,64.
• Greater the word length more powerful the computer.
2. Speed
• Computers can calculate at very high speeds.
• Microcomputer  can execute millions of  instructions per second without any mistake.
• Supercomputers can operate at speeds measured in nanoseconds or in picoseconds- one thousand to one 
million times faster than microcomputers.
3.   Storage
• Computers have main memory and auxiliary memory systems.
• Auxiliary storage devices are capable of  storing huge amounts of  data.
• Information that the user wants can be retrieved in few seconds.

CHARACTERISTICS OF COMPUTERS
4. Accuracy
• Accuracy of  a computer system is very high.
• Errors in hardware can occur but error detecting and correcting techniques will prevent false results.
• In most cases errors are due to human factor rather than technological flaws.
5. Versatility
• Computers can perform activities ranging from simple calculations to navigating missiles and satellites.
• They are capable of  performing almost any task.
• They can communicate with other computers.
6. Automation
• Once a task is initiated computers can proceed on its own till its completion.
• They execute programs in correct sequence, switch on or switch off  machines at correct time etc.
7. Diligence
• Computers perform tasks irrespective  of  whether it is interesting, creative, boring etc.
• Do not have any human weaknesses


## Page 7
TYPES OF COMPUTERS
Microcomputer:
• Small, single user computers based on a microprocessor. They have keyboard monitors, storage devices.
• They are further classified as personal computers, workstations, portable computers etc.
Minicomputer:
• Is a multiuser computer capable of  supporting ten to hundreds of  users simultaneously.
Mainframe:
• Powerful multiuser computers capable of  supporting hundreds of  users simultaneously.
• Used by banks, airline systems etc.
Supercomputer.
• Extremely fast computer.
• Used by government agencies
• These machines are for applications requiring very large programs and huge amounts of  data that must 
be processed quickly
• The main difference between a supercomputer and a mainframe is that a supercomputer channels 
all its power into executing a few programs as fast as possible, whereas a mainframe uses its 
power to execute many programs concurrently.
